## Description

On September 5, 2024, one or two days ago,the European Commission, on behalf of the European Union, signed the Council of Europe Framework Convention on Artificial Intelligence, Human Rights, Democracy, and the Rule of Law, marking the birth of the world’s first legally binding international convention on artificial intelligence. This historic event took place during the informal conference of Ministers of Justice of the Council of Europe member states in Vilnius, Lithuania, with European Commission Vice-President for Values and Transparency, Věra Jourová, signing the Convention.

This Convention represents a significant step forward in the global governance of artificial intelligence, ensuring that AI development aligns with human rights, democracy, and the rule of law. It sets a foundation for the safe, transparent, and trustworthy development of AI technologies worldwide.

Technological Significance:

The Convention is fully aligned with the EU AI Act and emphasizes the following key principles:

Human-Centric AI: Ensuring that AI technologies are consistent with the principles of human rights, democracy, and the rule of law.
Risk-Based Approach: Adopting a differentiated risk management strategy to ensure that AI systems throughout their lifecycle have a positive impact on society.
Transparency and Accountability: Strengthening the transparency, robustness, and safety of AI systems, ensuring traceability and accountability for AI activities.
Support for Innovation: Promoting safe innovation through mechanisms such as regulatory sandboxes, allowing for the testing and development of AI technologies in a controlled environment.
