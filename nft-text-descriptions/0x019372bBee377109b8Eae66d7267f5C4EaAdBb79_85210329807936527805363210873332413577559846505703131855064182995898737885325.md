## A. Classification of Dominant Attitudes （主要态度分类）  
- **Optimistic (乐观)**: Roughly 10–20% of people voice strongly optimistic views, believing AGI/ASI will usher in great benefits for humanity. This group highlights potential breakthroughs in science, medicine, and quality of life, often expressing excitement that superintelligent AI could **solve problems humans cannot** ([Hope or horror? The great AI debate dividing its pioneers | Technology | The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2023/oct/24/hope-or-horror-the-great-ai-debate-dividing-its-pioneers#:~:text=Hassabis%20says%20he%20would%20have,%E2%80%9D)). For example, some tech leaders predict AI’s impact will be “very or somewhat positive” in coming decades ([How the US Public and AI Experts View Artificial Intelligence | Pew Research Center](https://www.pewresearch.org/internet/2025/04/03/how-the-us-public-and-ai-experts-view-artificial-intelligence/#:~:text=Experts%20are%20far%20more%20positive,vs.%2017)). *(这一群体约占10–20%，对AGI/ASI持强烈乐观态度，认为其将为人类带来巨大益处。他们强调AI可能带来的科学、医疗和生活质量突破，对超级智能有望解决人类无法攻克的问题充满兴奋 ([Hope or horror? The great AI debate dividing its pioneers | Technology | The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2023/oct/24/hope-or-horror-the-great-ai-debate-dividing-its-pioneers#:~:text=Hassabis%20says%20he%20would%20have,%E2%80%9D))。例如，一些科技领袖预测AI在未来几十年将带来“大体正面”的影响 ([How the US Public and AI Experts View Artificial Intelligence | Pew Research Center](https://www.pewresearch.org/internet/2025/04/03/how-the-us-public-and-ai-experts-view-artificial-intelligence/#:~:text=Experts%20are%20far%20more%20positive,vs.%2017))。)*

- **Pessimistic (悲观)**: A significant share – often around **40–50%** in surveys – leans pessimistic, voicing **concern or fear** about advanced AI. For instance, over half of Americans (52%) say they are **more concerned than excited** about AI’s growing role ([Public Opinion | The 2024 AI Index Report | Stanford HAI](https://hai.stanford.edu/ai-index/2024-ai-index-report/public-opinion#:~:text=A%20survey%20from%20Ipsos%20shows,in%202022)) ([How the US Public and AI Experts View Artificial Intelligence | Pew Research Center](https://www.pewresearch.org/internet/2025/04/03/how-the-us-public-and-ai-experts-view-artificial-intelligence/#:~:text=And%20while%2047,among%20the%20public)). Common worries include job losses, loss of control, or even existential dangers. Some fear AGI could pose an **“extinction” threat comparable to nuclear war or pandemics** if mismanaged ([Hope or horror? The great AI debate dividing its pioneers | Technology | The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2023/oct/24/hope-or-horror-the-great-ai-debate-dividing-its-pioneers#:~:text=Demis%20Hassabis%20says%20he%20is,to%20pandemics%20or%20nuclear%20weapons)). *(相当大比例的人持悲观态度——调查中往往约有40–50%的人对高级AI表示担忧或恐惧。例如，美国有超过一半民众（52%）表示他们对AI越来越普及“**更多是担忧而非兴奋**” ([Public Opinion | The 2024 AI Index Report | Stanford HAI](https://hai.stanford.edu/ai-index/2024-ai-index-report/public-opinion#:~:text=A%20survey%20from%20Ipsos%20shows,in%202022)) ([How the US Public and AI Experts View Artificial Intelligence | Pew Research Center](https://www.pewresearch.org/internet/2025/04/03/how-the-us-public-and-ai-experts-view-artificial-intelligence/#:~:text=And%20while%2047,among%20the%20public))。常见忧虑包括就业流失、对AI失去控制，甚至存在风险。部分人担心如果管理不当，AGI可能带来类似核战或瘟疫般的“**灭绝**”威胁 ([Hope or horror? The great AI debate dividing its pioneers | Technology | The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2023/oct/24/hope-or-horror-the-great-ai-debate-dividing-its-pioneers#:~:text=Demis%20Hassabis%20says%20he%20is,to%20pandemics%20or%20nuclear%20weapons))。)*

- **Neutral or Uncertain (中立/观望)**: A smaller segment (perhaps ~10–20%) remains neutral or unsure. These individuals neither hype AI as salvation nor doom; instead they **adopt a wait-and-see approach**. Surveys indicate many people fall in between optimism and fear – for example, about one-third of the U.S. public say they are equally excited and concerned, or simply “not sure” about AI’s impact ([How the US Public and AI Experts View Artificial Intelligence | Pew Research Center](https://www.pewresearch.org/internet/2025/04/03/how-the-us-public-and-ai-experts-view-artificial-intelligence/#:~:text=%2876,them%20personally)). *(较小的群体（约占10–20%）保持中立或尚未确定立场。他们既不将AI吹捧为救世主，也不视其为灾难，而是**采取观望态度**。调查显示，许多人介于乐观与恐惧之间——例如，大约三分之一的美国公众表示他们对AI的影响“同样兴奋与担忧”，或者干脆“说不清” ([How the US Public and AI Experts View Artificial Intelligence | Pew Research Center](https://www.pewresearch.org/internet/2025/04/03/how-the-us-public-and-ai-experts-view-artificial-intelligence/#:~:text=%2876,them%20personally))。)*

- **Ambivalent (复杂矛盾)**: Perhaps the largest category consists of those with **mixed feelings** – simultaneously hopeful *and* anxious. This “middle way” perspective is exemplified by AI pioneers who acknowledge a gap between **“hope and horror”** ([Hope or horror? The great AI debate dividing its pioneers | Technology | The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2023/oct/24/hope-or-horror-the-great-ai-debate-dividing-its-pioneers#:~:text=Demis%20Hassabis%20says%20he%20is,to%20pandemics%20or%20nuclear%20weapons)) ([Hope or horror? The great AI debate dividing its pioneers | Technology | The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2023/oct/24/hope-or-horror-the-great-ai-debate-dividing-its-pioneers#:~:text=%E2%80%9CI%20am%20not%20in%20the,base%20in%20King%E2%80%99s%20Cross%2C%20London)). They believe AI *“can go well but we’ve got to be active in shaping that”* ([Hope or horror? The great AI debate dividing its pioneers | Technology | The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2023/oct/24/hope-or-horror-the-great-ai-debate-dividing-its-pioneers#:~:text=%E2%80%9CI%20am%20not%20in%20the,base%20in%20King%E2%80%99s%20Cross%2C%20London)). Many people echo this duality: excited by AI’s promises (efficiency, innovation) yet worried about its risks (ethics, safety). As one AI CEO put it, the future could be *“fundamentally positive”* if risks are managed – but the **same innovations could go quite wrong** if not handled responsibly ([Dario Amodei — Machines of Loving Grace](https://darioamodei.com/machines-of-loving-grace#:~:text=I%20think%20and%20talk%20a,bad%20the%20risks%20could%20be)) ([OpenAI CEO warns Senate: 'If this technology goes wrong, it can go quite wrong' - ABC News](https://abcnews.go.com/Business/openai-ceo-warns-senate-technology-wrong-wrong/story?id=99357748#:~:text=OpenAI%20CEO%20Sam%20Altman%20,falls%20into%20a%20downward%20spiral)). *(抱有**矛盾复杂心态**的人可能是最大的类别——他们同时怀有希望和焦虑。这种“中间道路”的观点可从AI先驱者的表态中体现：他们承认在**“希望与恐怖”**之间存在落差 ([Hope or horror? The great AI debate dividing its pioneers | Technology | The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2023/oct/24/hope-or-horror-the-great-ai-debate-dividing-its-pioneers#:~:text=Demis%20Hassabis%20says%20he%20is,to%20pandemics%20or%20nuclear%20weapons)) ([Hope or horror? The great AI debate dividing its pioneers | Technology | The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2023/oct/24/hope-or-horror-the-great-ai-debate-dividing-its-pioneers#:~:text=%E2%80%9CI%20am%20not%20in%20the,base%20in%20King%E2%80%99s%20Cross%2C%20London))，相信AI“**可以带来美好结果，但我们必须积极塑造**” ([Hope or horror? The great AI debate dividing its pioneers | Technology | The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2023/oct/24/hope-or-horror-the-great-ai-debate-dividing-its-pioneers#:~:text=%E2%80%9CI%20am%20not%20in%20the,base%20in%20King%E2%80%99s%20Cross%2C%20London))。许多民众也有这种双重心态：一方面对AI的前景（效率、创新）感到兴奋，另一方面又对其风险（伦理、安全）深感忧虑。正如一位AI公司CEO所言，如果控制得当，未来可能“**从根本上是积极的**”，但同样的技术创新**如果处理不当也可能出大问题** ([Dario Amodei — Machines of Loving Grace](https://darioamodei.com/machines-of-loving-grace#:~:text=I%20think%20and%20talk%20a,bad%20the%20risks%20could%20be)) ([OpenAI CEO warns Senate: 'If this technology goes wrong, it can go quite wrong' - ABC News](https://abcnews.go.com/Business/openai-ceo-warns-senate-technology-wrong-wrong/story?id=99357748#:~:text=OpenAI%20CEO%20Sam%20Altman%20,falls%20into%20a%20downward%20spiral))。)*

The great AI debate dividing its pioneers | Technology | The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2023/oct/24/hope-or-horror-the-great-ai-debate-dividing-its-pioneers#:~:text=Demis%20Hassabis%20says%20he%20is,to%20pandemics%20or%20nuclear%20weapons))。但与此同时，不少专家呼吁在恐慌与乐观间寻求*平衡*：他们强调如果有适当的研究与保障措施，AI对人类将是“根本上积极的” ([Dario Amodei — Machines of Loving Grace](https://darioamodei.com/machines-of-loving-grace#:~:text=I%20think%20and%20talk%20a,bad%20the%20risks%20could%20be))。总之，**专家对AI的益处抱有希望，但也以审慎呼吁来节制乐观**，并且往往带头推动本领域的安全措施和伦理规范。)*

**General Public** – **Curious but anxious:** Among everyday people worldwide, feelings about AGI are mixed but skew toward **caution**. With the 2023–2024 AI boom, public awareness of AI soared – 63% of global respondents had heard of ChatGPT by late 2023 ([Public Opinion | The 2024 AI Index Report | Stanford HAI](https://hai.stanford.edu/ai-index/2024-ai-index-report/public-opinion#:~:text=ChatGPT%20is%20widely%20known%20and,widely%20used)). This familiarity bred both fascination and nervousness. Polls find that a **majority of the public feels wary**: for example, 52% of Americans (and similar shares in Europe) report being more worried than excited about AI’s growing presence ([Public Opinion | The 2024 AI Index Report | Stanford HAI](https://hai.stanford.edu/ai-index/2024-ai-index-report/public-opinion#:~:text=A%20survey%20from%20Ipsos%20shows,in%202022)) ([How the US Public and AI Experts View Artificial Intelligence | Pew Research Center](https://www.pewresearch.org/internet/2025/04/03/how-the-us-public-and-ai-experts-view-artificial-intelligence/#:~:text=And%20while%2047,among%20the%20public)). Specific fears include **economic displacement** (job loss due to automation) – indeed 64% of U.S. adults expect AI to eliminate more jobs than it creates ([How the US Public and AI Experts View Artificial Intelligence | Pew Research Center](https://www.pewresearch.org/internet/2025/04/03/how-the-us-public-and-ai-experts-view-artificial-intelligence/#:~:text=match%20at%20L345%20loss,over%20the%20next%2020%20years)) – and erosion of trust (61% say they anticipate AI will make it harder to tell what’s real or fake) ([2023 was the year we toyed with artificial intelligence - Santa Monica Daily Press](https://smdp.com/news/2023-the-year-we-played-with-artificial-intelligence-and-werent-sure-what-to-do-about-it/#:~:text=By%20spring%2C%20AI,footage%20in%20Ukraine%20and%20Gaza)). At the same time, many laypeople are intrigued by AI’s capabilities. The public’s use of AI tools skyrocketed, and anecdotes of AI-assisted creativity or productivity are common. This leads to a prevalent **ambivalence**: people appreciate practical benefits (like AI in healthcare or education) but harbor a background fear of “science-fiction” scenarios (rogue AI, loss of human control). In summary, **the average citizen’s stance in 2024–25 is one of cautious curiosity** – a mix of optimism about AI’s conveniences and deep concern about its risks and societal impacts.

*（**普通公众**——**好奇但焦虑：**在全球范围内，普通民众对AGI的态度错综复杂，但整体上偏向**谨慎**。随着2023–2024年AI热潮的到来，公众对AI的认知迅速提升——到2023年底，63%的全球受访者已听说过ChatGPT ([Public Opinion | The 2024 AI Index Report | Stanford HAI](https://hai.stanford.edu/ai-index/2024-ai-index-report/public-opinion#:~:text=ChatGPT%20is%20widely%20known%20and,widely%20used))。这种熟悉既带来了着迷，也引发了紧张不安。民调显示**大多数公众持警惕态度**：例如，52%的美国人（欧洲民众情况类似）表示对于AI日益扩大应用，他们“担忧多于兴奋” ([Public Opinion | The 2024 AI Index Report | Stanford HAI](https://hai.stanford.edu/ai-index/2024-ai-index-report/public-opinion#:~:text=A%20survey%20from%20Ipsos%20shows,in%202022)) ([How the US Public and AI Experts View Artificial Intelligence | Pew Research Center](https://www.pewresearch.org/internet/2025/04/03/how-the-us-public-and-ai-experts-view-artificial-intelligence/#:~:text=And%20while%2047,among%20the%20public))。具体恐惧包括**经济替代**（担心自动化导致失业）——的确有64%的美国成年人预计AI将使工作岗位净减少 ([How the US Public and AI Experts View Artificial Intelligence | Pew Research Center](https://www.pewresearch.org/internet/2025/04/03/how-the-us-public-and-ai-experts-view-artificial-intelligence/#:~:text=match%20at%20L345%20loss,over%20the%20next%2020%20years))——以及信任流失（61%的人预期AI会令真假难辨的情况加剧） ([2023 was the year we toyed with artificial intelligence - Santa Monica Daily Press](https://smdp.com/news/2023-the-year-we-played-with-artificial-intelligence-and-werent-sure-what-to-do-about-it/#:~:text=By%20spring%2C%20AI,footage%20in%20Ukraine%20and%20Gaza))。与此同时，许多普通人也对AI的强大能力感到好奇。公众使用AI工具的频率激增，AI辅助创造或提升效率的案例屡见不鲜。这导致一种普遍的**矛盾心理**：人们一方面欣赏AI在日常便利（如医疗、教育）方面带来的好处，但另一方面又隐隐担心那些“科幻式”的场景（失控AI、人类失去主导）。总的来说，**2024–25年普通民众的立场是谨慎的好奇**——既对AI带来的便利保持乐观，又深切担忧其风险和社会影响，两种情绪并存。)*

**Policymakers & Governments** – **Guarded and proactive:**  Leaders in government and policy have responded to AGI with a mix of **hopeful ambition and preventative caution**. On one hand, officials recognize AI’s strategic importance – many governments see AI as key to economic growth and even geopolitical strength. This has led to national AI initiatives aiming to **harness the benefits** of advanced AI. For example, China’s government emphasizes “AI for good” and innovation, investing heavily in AI while also issuing an **AI Safety Governance Framework** in 2024 to manage risks ([China-releases-AI-safety-governance-framework | DLA Piper](https://www.dlapiper.com/en-us/insights/publications/2024/09/china-releases-ai-safety-governance-framework#:~:text=the%20Global%20AI%20Governance%20Initiative,%E2%80%9D)) ([China-releases-AI-safety-governance-framework | DLA Piper](https://www.dlapiper.com/en-us/insights/publications/2024/09/china-releases-ai-safety-governance-framework#:~:text=Principles%20and%20framework%20for%20AI,safety%20governance)). Western policymakers likewise talk about AI’s promise for productivity and public services. On the other hand, 2023–2025 saw a notable rise in official concern about **AI risks**. Policymakers have held hearings and high-level meetings on scenarios from mass unemployment to existential threats. In the U.S., Congress heard warnings that **“if this technology goes wrong, it can go quite wrong”** ([OpenAI CEO warns Senate: 'If this technology goes wrong, it can go quite wrong' - ABC News](https://abcnews.go.com/Business/openai-ceo-warns-senate-technology-wrong-wrong/story?id=99357748#:~:text=OpenAI%20CEO%20Sam%20Altman%20,falls%20into%20a%20downward%20spiral)), and in late 2024 the White House issued the first-ever **National Security Memorandum on AI**, stressing safe deployment of AI in defense and intelligence ([The White House's Big Vision of AI Safety: Building a Responsive ...](https://www.rand.org/pubs/commentary/2024/11/the-white-houses-big-vision-of-ai-safety-building-a.html#:~:text=The%20White%20House%27s%20Big%20Vision,diverse%20set%20of%20issues%2C)). The EU moved even faster: by the end of 2023, negotiators in Brussels hashed out a deal for the **world’s first major AI law (EU AI Act)**, imposing legal safeguards on high-risk AI systems ([2023 was the year we toyed with artificial intelligence - Santa Monica Daily Press](https://smdp.com/news/2023-the-year-we-played-with-artificial-intelligence-and-werent-sure-what-to-do-about-it/#:~:text=San%20Francisco%20startup%20OpenAI%2C%20nearly,first%20major%20AI%20legal%20safeguards)). At the UK’s global **AI Safety Summit** (Nov 2023), government delegates from 28 countries jointly acknowledged the potential **existential risks** of future AI and the need for international coordination on AI governance ([AI risk must be treated as seriously as climate crisis, says Google DeepMind chief | Technology | The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2023/oct/24/ai-risk-climate-crisis-google-deepmind-chief-demis-hassabis-regulation#:~:text=Hassabis%2C%20the%20British%20chief%20executive,intelligent%20systems)). In sum, **policymakers are increasingly engaged and cautious** – they often sound optimistic about AI’s economic and social upsides, but this is coupled with public calls for “responsible AI” and new regulations to forestall the worst-case outcomes.

*（**政策制定者和政府**——**审慎且积极：**各国政府领导人和政策制定者对于AGI表现出**雄心与预防并重**的态度。一方面，官员们认可AI的战略重要性——许多政府将AI视为经济增长乃至地缘实力的关键。这推动各国出台AI发展计划，旨在**利用先进AI的益处**。例如，中国政府强调“以人为本、AI向善”的理念，在大力投入AI研发的同时，于2024年发布**《人工智能安全治理框架》**以管控风险 ([China-releases-AI-safety-governance-framework | DLA Piper](https://www.dlapiper.com/en-us/insights/publications/2024/09/china-releases-ai-safety-governance-framework#:~:text=the%20Global%20AI%20Governance%20Initiative,%E2%80%9D)) ([China-releases-AI-safety-governance-framework | DLA Piper](https://www.dlapiper.com/en-us/insights/publications/2024/09/china-releases-ai-safety-governance-framework#:~:text=Principles%20and%20framework%20for%20AI,safety%20governance))。西方国家的政策制定者同样谈及AI在提升生产力和公共服务方面的前景。另一方面，2023–2025年官方对**AI风险**的关注显著上升。各国政界密集举行听证会和高层会议，讨论从大规模失业到生存威胁等情景。在美国国会听证中，议员们听取了**“如果这项技术出岔子，可能会非常糟糕”**的警示 ([OpenAI CEO warns Senate: 'If this technology goes wrong, it can go quite wrong' - Santa Monica Daily Press](https://smdp.com/news/2023-the-year-we-played-with-artificial-intelligence-and-werent-sure-what-to-do-about-it/#:~:text=San%20Francisco%20startup%20OpenAI%2C%20nearly,first%20major%20AI%20legal%20safeguards))。在英国于2023年11月主办的全球**AI安全峰会**上，28国政府代表联合承认未来AI可能带来的**生存风险**，呼吁在AI治理上开展国际协作 ([AI risk must be treated as seriously as climate crisis, says Google DeepMind chief | Technology | The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2023/oct/24/ai-risk-climate-crisis-google-deepmind-chief-demis-hassabis-regulation#:~:text=Hassabis%2C%20the%20British%20chief%20executive,intelligent%20systems))。总之，**政策制定者正日益积极且保持谨慎**——他们往往对AI在经济社会方面的好处持乐观态度，但同时公开呼吁“负责任的AI”，并制定新规以防范最坏情形。)*

**Artists and Creatives** – **Inspired *and* threatened:** The artistic community’s relationship with AI is emotionally charged and divided. **Many artists are alarmed** by generative AI’s ability to mimic creative styles, seeing it as a threat to human originality and livelihoods. In 2023, as image- and text-generating models became widespread, artists and writers experienced real impacts – AI-generated art and prose began competing with human work. This sparked backlash: visual artists noticed AI copies of their styles and warned that such AI **“now threatens the livelihood of artists worldwide”** ([Visual artists fight back against artificial intelligence companies for repurposing their work | PBS News](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/arts/visual-artists-fight-back-against-artificial-intelligence-companies-for-repurposing-their-work#:~:text=The%20word%20%E2%80%9Chuman%E2%80%9D%20has%20a,that%E2%80%99s%20decidedly%20not%20human%3A%C2%A0artificial%20intelligence)). Lawsuits were filed (e.g. against Stability AI and Midjourney) by artists claiming these tools violated their copyrights and profited from their creations ([Visual artists fight back against artificial intelligence companies for repurposing their work | PBS News](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/arts/visual-artists-fight-back-against-artificial-intelligence-companies-for-repurposing-their-work#:~:text=McKernan%20is%20now%20one%20of,can%20generate%C2%A0new%20imagery%20on%20command)) ([Visual artists fight back against artificial intelligence companies for repurposing their work | PBS News](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/arts/visual-artists-fight-back-against-artificial-intelligence-companies-for-repurposing-their-work#:~:text=The%20suit%20alleges%20that%20the,that%20compete%20against%20the%20originals)). Hollywood writers and actors went on strike in 2023 partly over fears that studios would use AI to generate scripts or digital actors, underscoring a widespread fear of **creative displacement** ([2023 was the year we toyed with artificial intelligence - Santa Monica Daily Press](https://smdp.com/news/2023-the-year-we-played-with-artificial-intelligence-and-werent-sure-what-to-do-about-it/#:~:text=Worries%20escalated%20as%20this%20new,visual%20artists%20and%20bestselling%20authors)). At the same time, **some creators embrace AI as a new medium**. Innovative artists experiment with AI to generate music, imagery and poetry, treating it as a collaborative tool. They argue that AI can enhance human creativity rather than replace it – for example, by generating ideas or styles for an artist to build upon. This positive outlook sees AI as opening up new artistic frontiers (interactive art, personalized entertainment) and democratizing creation for those without formal training. Across the arts, one finds a spectrum: from purists who insist **“AI art” isn’t true art and must be controlled**, to enthusiasts who find it **“thrilling”** and full of creative potential ([AI is no threat to traditional artists. But it is thrilling.](https://www.washingtonpost.com/arts-entertainment/2023/02/15/ai-in-art/#:~:text=thrilling,%C2%B7%20Why)). **Emotionally, artists are torn** – they marvel at AI’s capabilities yet passionately defend the irreplaceable value of human creativity. As one group of illustrators stated, they “are not inherently opposed to AI” but demand it **not exploit or eclipse human artists** ([Visual artists fight back against artificial intelligence companies for repurposing their work | PBS News](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/arts/visual-artists-fight-back-against-artificial-intelligence-companies-for-repurposing-their-work#:~:text=The%20suit%20alleges%20that%20the,that%20compete%20against%20the%20originals)). This push-and-pull is driving an artistic reflection on what it means to be creative in the age of AI, giving rise to myriad works *about* AI (from dystopian sci-fi films to AI-inspired digital art) that capture both wonder and dread of AGI.

*（**艺术家和创意群体**——**既受启发又受威胁：**艺术界与AI的关系充满情感张力，也存在分歧。**许多艺术家感到警觉**，因为生成式AI能够模仿创作风格，在他们看来这威胁到人类原创性和生计。2023年，随着图像和文本生成模型的普及，艺术家和作家切实感受到影响——AI生成的艺术品和文章开始与人类作品竞争。这引发了反弹：视觉艺术家发现了模仿自己风格的AI作品，并警告这种AI**“现如今已对全世界艺术家的生计构成威胁”** ([Visual artists fight back against artificial intelligence companies for repurposing their work | PBS News](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/arts/visual-artists-fight-back-against-artificial-intelligence-companies-for-repurposing-their-work#:~:text=The%20word%20%E2%80%9Chuman%E2%80%9D%20has%20a,that%E2%80%99s%20decidedly%20not%20human%3A%C2%A0artificial%20intelligence))。一些艺术家提起了诉讼（如针对Stability AI和Midjourney），指控这些工具侵犯版权、利用人类创作牟利 ([Visual artists fight back against artificial intelligence companies for repurposing their work | PBS News](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/arts/visual-artists-fight-back-against-artificial-intelligence-companies-for-repurposing-their-work#:~:text=McKernan%20is%20now%20one%20of,can%20generate%C2%A0new%20imagery%20on%20command)) ([Visual artists fight back against artificial intelligence companies for repurposing their work | PBS News](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/arts/visual-artists-fight-back-against-artificial-intelligence-companies-for-repurposing-their-work#:~:text=The%20suit%20alleges%20that%20the,that%20compete%20against%20the%20originals))。2023年，好莱坞编剧和演员也因担心制片厂用AI生成剧本或数字演员而罢工，凸显了**创意被取代**的普遍恐惧 ([2023 was the year we toyed with artificial intelligence - Santa Monica Daily Press](https://smdp.com/news/2023-the-year-we-played-with-artificial-intelligence-and-werent-sure-what-to-do-about-it/#:~:text=Worries%20escalated%20as%20this%20new,visual%20artists%20and%20bestselling%20authors))。但与此同时，**也有部分创作者将AI视为新的创作媒介并欣然接受**。一些先锋艺术家尝试用AI创作音乐、图像和诗歌，视其为协作工具。他们认为AI可以增强而非取代人类创意——例如提供灵感或风格供艺术家再创作。这种积极观点认为AI开启了新的艺术前沿（互动艺术、个性化娱乐），并让缺乏正规训练的人也能参与创作。在艺术界，存在一个光谱：一端是坚持认为**“AI艺术不是真正的艺术，必须加以限制”**的纯粹主义者，另一端是觉得AI非常**“令人兴奋”**且富有创造潜力的拥趸 ([AI is no threat to traditional artists. But it is thrilling.](https://www.washingtonpost.com/arts-entertainment/2023/02/15/ai-in-art/#:~:text=thrilling,%C2%B7%20Why))。**情感上，艺术家们内心矛盾**——他们一方面惊叹于AI的能力，另一方面又极力捍卫人类创意无法替代的价值。正如一群插画师所言，他们“并非天生反对AI”，但要求AI**不得剥削或取代人类艺术家** ([Visual artists fight back against artificial intelligence companies for repurposing their work | PBS News](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/arts/visual-artists-fight-back-against-artificial-intelligence-companies-for-repurposing-their-work#:~:text=The%20suit%20alleges%20that%20the,that%20compete%20against%20the%20originals))。这种拉锯正在促使艺术界反思在AI时代创造力的意义，并催生出大量*关于*AI的艺术作品（从反乌托邦科幻电影到AI灵感的数码艺术），既传达了对AGI的惊奇，也透露出隐忧。)*

## D. Top 10 Common Hopes vs. Fears about AGI/ASI （十大常见希望与十大常见恐惧）

**Top 10 Hopes (积极展望)** – Humanity’s hopes for AGI/ASI often focus on its potential to **solve problems and elevate civilization**:  

1. **Medical Breakthroughs and Cures:** Superintelligent AI could decode diseases and develop cures for cancer, Alzheimer’s, and other illnesses. Many hope AGI will *“help us cure diseases we currently can’t,”* spurring an **“amazing revolution in healthcare”** ([Five ways AI could improve the world: ‘We can cure all diseases, stabilise our climate, halt poverty’ | Artificial intelligence (AI) | The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2023/jul/06/ai-artificial-intelligence-world-diseases-climate-scenarios-experts#:~:text=%E2%80%98There%20is%20going%20to%20be,an%20amazing%20revolution%20in%20healthcare%E2%80%99)). (医疗突破与治愈疾病：人们期待超级智能AI破解疾病难题，开发出癌症、阿尔茨海默症等的疗法。许多人希望AGI将“**帮助我们治愈目前束手无策的疾病**”，引发“**医疗保健的惊人革命**” ([Five ways AI could improve the world: ‘We can cure all diseases, stabilise our climate, halt poverty’ | Artificial intelligence (AI) | The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2023/jul/06/ai-artificial-intelligence-world-diseases-climate-scenarios-experts#:~:text=%E2%80%98There%20is%20going%20to%20be,an%20amazing%20revolution%20in%20healthcare%E2%80%99))。)

2. **Ending Poverty and Boosting Prosperity:** Advanced AI might greatly increase productivity and generate wealth, enabling abundance for all. Optimists imagine AGI automating drudgery and driving costs down so that **no one lacks basic needs**. Some even foresee AI contributing to funding a universal basic income, “sharing the upsides” of AI-driven growth ([Hope or horror? The great AI debate dividing its pioneers | Technology | The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2023/oct/24/hope-or-horror-the-great-ai-debate-dividing-its-pioneers#:~:text=He%20says%20AI%20advances%20will,transport%20and%20accommodation%20for%20free)). (消除贫困、促进繁荣：先进AI可能大幅提高生产力并创造财富，为全民带来充裕。乐观者设想AGI自动处理繁琐劳动、降低各类成本，从而**消除基本生活匮乏**。有人甚至预见AI将助力提供全民基本收入，实现AI驱动增长带来的“**红利共享**” ([Hope or horror? The great AI debate dividing its pioneers | Technology | The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2023/oct/24/hope-or-horror-the-great-ai-debate-dividing-its-pioneers#:~:text=He%20says%20AI%20advances%20will,transport%20and%20accommodation%20for%20free))。)

3. **Climate Change Solutions:** AGI is hoped to help humanity **stabilize the climate and restore the environment**. This includes breakthroughs in clean energy, carbon capture, and climate modeling. Experts note AI can already speed up climate and weather simulations ([Five ways AI could improve the world: ‘We can cure all diseases, stabilise our climate, halt poverty’ | Artificial intelligence (AI) | The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2023/jul/06/ai-artificial-intelligence-world-diseases-climate-scenarios-experts#:~:text=harm%20is%20a%20distraction%20from,we%20are%20using%20it%20now)), and a superintelligence might optimize our response to global warming in time to avert disaster. (气候变化解决方案：人们希望AGI帮助人类**稳定气候、恢复环境**，包括清洁能源、碳捕获和气候建模方面的突破。有专家指出AI已经能够加快气候和天气模型运算 ([Five ways AI could improve the world: ‘We can cure all diseases, stabilise our climate, halt poverty’ | Artificial intelligence (AI) | The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2023/jul/06/ai-artificial-intelligence-world-diseases-climate-scenarios-experts#:~:text=harm%20is%20a%20distraction%20from,we%20are%20using%20it%20now))；一个超级智能或许能优化我们应对全球变暖的方案，为人类抢得避免灾难的先机。)

4. **Scientific and Technological Enlightenment:** There is hope that an AGI could rapidly advance our scientific understanding – from physics to biology – ushering in a new era of discovery. An AGI might unravel mysteries of the universe (e.g. quantum gravity, extraterrestrial life) or invent technologies far beyond current human capability. Such an **“acceleration of technological progress”** could raise humanity to new heights ([Five ways AI could improve the world: ‘We can cure all diseases, stabilise our climate, halt poverty’ | Artificial intelligence (AI) | The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2023/jul/06/ai-artificial-intelligence-world-diseases-climate-scenarios-experts#:~:text=%E2%80%98AI%20could%20radically%20accelerate%20the,process%20of%20technological%20progress%20itself%E2%80%99)). (科学与技术的飞跃：人们寄望AGI能迅速推进人类在物理、生物等领域的科学理解，开启发现的新时代。AGI或可解开宇宙之谜（如量子引力、地外生命），或发明出远超当前人类能力的技术。这种**“技术进步的加速”**将使人类迈向新的高度 ([Five ways AI could improve the world: ‘We can cure all diseases, stabilise our climate, halt poverty’ | Artificial intelligence (AI) | The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2023/jul/06/ai-artificial-intelligence-world-diseases-climate-scenarios-experts#:~:text=%E2%80%98AI%20could%20radically%20accelerate%20the,process%20of%20technological%20progress%20itself%E2%80%99))。)

5. **Education and Knowledge for All:** AI tutors and personalized education systems driven by AGI could **democratize learning worldwide**. A top hope is for AI to act as an always-available teacher – tailoring lessons to each student’s needs – thereby dramatically raising global literacy and skill levels. Tech philanthropists predict that within years, AI-powered agents will *“tutor students… and much more”* in ways previously unimaginable ([AI-powered agents are the future of computing | Bill Gates](https://www.gatesnotes.com/meet-bill/tech-thinking/reader/ai-agents#:~:text=URL%3A%20https%3A%2F%2Fwww.gatesnotes.com%2Fmeet,Bill%20Gates)). (教育与知识全民共享：由AGI驱动的AI导师和个性化教育系统可**使全世界获得平等学习机会**。一大期望是让AI充当随时在线的教师——因材施教地辅导每个学生，从而显著提高全球教育和技能水平。科技慈善家预测，不出几年，AI代理将以难以想象的方式“**辅导学生……以及更多**” ([AI-powered agents are the future of computing | Bill Gates](https://www.gatesnotes.com/meet-bill/tech-thinking/reader/ai-agents#:~:text=URL%3A%20https%3A%2F%2Fwww.gatesnotes.com%2Fmeet,Bill%20Gates))。)

6. **Augmenting Human Abilities:** Rather than replace humans, AGI could **augment our abilities** – serving as a cognitive partner that boosts creativity, memory, and problem-solving. People hope for “centaur” scenarios where humans plus AI achieve feats neither could alone. For example, an AGI co-author could help researchers formulate ideas or artists compose novel masterpieces, enhancing (not eclipsing) human genius. (增强人类能力：AGI并非取代人类，而是**增强我们的能力**——作为提升创造力、记忆力和解决问题能力的思维伙伴。人们期盼“半人马”模式，即人类与AI协作可以实现任何一方单独都无法完成的壮举。例如，一个AGI联合作者可以帮助研究人员提出创想，或协助艺术家创作出全新杰作，是对人类才智的增强而非掩盖。)

7. **Liberation from Menial Work:** A common hope is that AI automation will free people from drudgery and dangerous tasks. AGI-guided robots might handle manual labor, mining, cleaning, etc., allowing humans to pursue more creative, meaningful endeavors. This harkens to a utopian vision where **everyone’s basic work is handled by machines**, and leisure or higher pursuits become the norm. (从粗活中解放：一个普遍的期望是AI自动化能使人们从繁重乏味和危险的工作中解放出来。AGI指导的机器人或可承担体力劳动、采矿、清洁等任务，使人类得以从事更具创造性和意义的事业。这有几分乌托邦色彩：**每个人的基本劳作都由机器完成**，休闲或更高层次的追求成为常态。)

8. **Improved Quality of Life and Personal Assistants:** Many look forward to AGI as an ultimate smart assistant managing daily hassles. An AGI in your home could cook, clean, organize, and provide childcare or eldercare with tireless efficiency and empathy. Life could become easier and more enjoyable, with AI optimizing everything from one’s health regimen to travel plans – a future where **mundane stress is minimized** and well-being maximized. (生活质量提升与个人助手：许多人期待AGI充当终极智能助手，打理日常杂务。一个家庭中的AGI可以高效又富有同理心地料理烹饪、清洁、安排事务，甚至照顾老人和小孩。生活将变得更轻松、更愉悦，AI可优化从健康计划到旅行安排的一切——在这样的未来，**琐事压力被降至最低**，福祉得到最大化。)

9. **Global Problem-Solving and Wisdom:** Optimistically, a superintelligent AI might offer guidance to humanity in solving entrenched global issues – from conflict resolution and equitable policy to disaster response. Some imagine AGI as a *“wise councilor”* that could help human leaders make decisions that are more rational, just, and farsighted, free of our biases. In essence, AGI could help us **govern ourselves better**, avoiding wars and crises through superior logic and planning. (全球难题的解决与智慧启迪：乐观设想中，一个超级智能AI或许能在解决顽固的全球问题上为人类提供指导——从冲突调解、公平政策制定到灾难应对。有人将AGI想象为*“智慧参谋”*，帮助人类领导人做出更加理性、公正、深谋远虑的决策，摆脱人类自身的偏见。本质上，AGI可以助我们**更好地治理人类社会**，通过更高明的逻辑和规划来避免战争和危机。)

10. **Ensuring Humanity’s Long-Term Survival and Exploration:** Finally, visionaries hope AGI will safeguard our future in the cosmic sense – protecting us from existential threats (asteroids, pandemics, etc.) and enabling exploration beyond Earth. An ASI (Artificial Superintelligence) could devise planetary defense systems or propel innovations for space travel and colonization. In this hopeful view, **AGI is a tool for human continuity**, helping us thrive for centuries or even millennia to come, potentially as an interstellar civilization ([Five ways AI could improve the world: ‘We can cure all diseases, stabilise our climate, halt poverty’ | Artificial intelligence (AI) | The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2023/jul/06/ai-artificial-intelligence-world-diseases-climate-scenarios-experts#:~:text=%E2%80%98We%20can%20flourish%2C%20not%20just,but%20for%20billions%20of%20years%E2%80%99)). (确保人类长远存续与宇宙探索：最后，富有远见的人期待AGI从宏观上保障我们物种的未来——保护人类免受生存威胁（小行星撞击、全球瘟疫等），并使深空探索成为可能。一个ASI（人工超级智能）或能设计行星防御系统，或推动太空旅行和星际殖民方面的创新。在这种乐观展望中，**AGI是人类延续的工具**，帮助我们在未来几个世纪乃至千禧年中蓬勃发展，甚至有望成为星际文明 ([Five ways AI could improve the world: ‘We can cure all diseases, stabilise our climate, halt poverty’ | Artificial intelligence (AI) | The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2023/jul/06/ai-artificial-intelligence-world-diseases-climate-scenarios-experts#:~:text=%E2%80%98We%20can%20flourish%2C%20not%20just,but%20for%20billions%20of%20years%E2%80%99))。)

**Top 10 Fears (主要恐惧)** – Alongside hopes, humanity grapples with profound fears about AGI/ASI, often warning that unchecked superintelligence could have **catastrophic consequences**:  

1. **Mass Unemployment and Social Upheaval:** The most immediate fear is that AI will displace millions of workers, leading to joblessness, inequality, and instability. A majority in many countries expect AI to cause **net job losses** ([How the US Public and AI Experts View Artificial Intelligence | Pew Research Center](https://www.pewresearch.org/internet/2025/04/03/how-the-us-public-and-ai-experts-view-artificial-intelligence/#:~:text=match%20at%20L345%20loss,over%20the%20next%2020%20years)). There is anxiety that whole industries (from trucking to accounting to creative work) could be automated, concentrating wealth in the hands of AI owners and leaving others without livelihoods. Such economic disruption could fuel social unrest or a “useless class” dilemma. (大规模失业与社会动荡：最直接的恐惧是AI将取代数以百万计的劳动者，导致失业、贫富悬殊和社会不稳定。许多国家的多数民众预计AI会造成**就业岗位净减少** ([How the US Public and AI Experts View Artificial Intelligence | Pew Research Center](https://www.pewresearch.org/internet/2025/04/03/how-the-us-public-and-ai-experts-view-artificial-intelligence/#:~:text=match%20at%20L345%20loss,over%20the%20next%2020%20years))。人们担心包括卡车运输、会计乃至创意行业在内的整个行业都可能被自动化，从而使财富集中于AI所有者手中，其他人大批失去生计。这种经济剧变可能引发社会动荡，出现“无用阶级”的困境。)

2. **AI Misinformation and Erosion of Truth:** There is widespread fear that AI will be used to generate **fake news, deepfakes, and propaganda** at enormous scale, undermining social trust and democracy. Already by 2023, AI-generated deepfake images and videos had begun infiltrating politics and conflicts, making it *“increasingly difficult to distinguish between real and fabricated”* events ([2023 was the year we toyed with artificial intelligence - Santa Monica Daily Press](https://smdp.com/news/2023-the-year-we-played-with-artificial-intelligence-and-werent-sure-what-to-do-about-it/#:~:text=By%20spring%2C%20AI,footage%20in%20Ukraine%20and%20Gaza)). An AGI might flood the internet with highly convincing falsehoods or manipulate public opinion, with grave consequences for elections, justice, and social cohesion. (AI造假信息泛滥、真相沦丧：人们普遍担心AI会被用来大规模生成**假新闻、深度伪造和宣传**，破坏社会信任和民主治理。到2023年，AI生成的深度伪造图像和视频已开始渗入政治和冲突领域，让人们*“越来越难辨别真实与虚假”* ([2023 was the year we toyed with artificial intelligence - Santa Monica Daily Press](https://smdp.com/news/2023-the-year-we-played-with-artificial-intelligence-and-werent-sure-what-to-do-about-it/#:~:text=By%20spring%2C%20AI,footage%20in%20Ukraine%20and%20Gaza))。一个AGI可能充斥互联网以高度逼真的虚假信息或操纵公众舆论，给选举、公正和社会凝聚力带来严重后果。)

3. **Bias, Discrimination, and Ethical Failures:** Another fear is that AI systems, including AGI, will perpetuate or amplify biases present in data, leading to unfair or harmful outcomes at scale. If a superintelligence inherits human prejudices (about race, gender, etc.) or flawed values, it could systematically discriminate or make unethical decisions. Concerns over AI bias and lack of transparency are already high – 66% of the public worry AI may produce biased or inaccurate outputs ([OpenAI CEO warns Senate: 'If this technology goes wrong, it can go quite wrong' - ABC News](https://abcnews.go.com/Business/openai-ceo-warns-senate-technology-wrong-wrong/story?id=99357748#:~:text=AI%20poses%20%27profound%20risks%27)). An unchecked AGI could make **deeply unjust decisions** (in hiring, policing, etc.) far faster than we could correct them. (偏见歧视与伦理失范：人们还担心AI系统（包括AGI）会延续或放大数据中固有的偏见，从而大规模地产生不公或有害结果。如果一个超级智能继承了人类的偏见（种族、性别等）或扭曲的价值观，它可能系统性地歧视某些群体或做出不道德决策。公众对AI偏见和不透明的担忧已很高——有66%的人担心AI可能产生有偏见或不准确的输出 ([OpenAI CEO warns Senate: 'If this technology goes wrong, it can go quite wrong' - ABC News](https://abcnews.go.com/Business/openai-ceo-warns-senate-technology-wrong-wrong/story?id=99357748#:~:text=AI%20poses%20%27profound%20risks%27))。一个失控的AGI可能在招聘、执法等方面做出**极不公正的决定**，其速度之快将使人类来不及纠正。)

4. **Loss of Privacy and Surveillance:** Advanced AI could empower an **Orwellian surveillance state** or corporate intrusion into personal lives. Fears abound that ubiquitous AI monitoring (via cameras, smart devices, internet data) will erode any remaining privacy. In the wrong hands, AGI might analyze every digital trace to profile and control individuals. This is a particular concern in societies already deploying AI surveillance – the specter of a superintelligent “Big Brother” that can watch and predict our every move is chilling to many. (隐私丧失与监控泛滥：先进AI可能助长**奥威尔式的监控社会**或企业对个人生活的入侵。人们非常担心无处不在的AI监控（通过摄像头、智能设备、网络数据）将摧毁残存的隐私。在不当之手中，AGI或能分析每一条数字痕迹来描绘和控制个人。这在已经部署AI监控的社会中尤为令人不安——一个能够监视并预测我们一举一动的超级智能“老大哥”的幽灵令许多人不寒而栗。)

5. **Malicious Use and AI-empowered Crime:** There is concern that criminals, terrorists, or rogue states will harness AI for nefarious ends – from **autonomous drones and cyberattacks** to generating novel bioweapons. An AGI could dramatically accelerate hacking or facilitate the design of deadly pathogens, far beyond human capabilities. Indeed, global AI leaders have warned that AI could aid the creation of bioweapons ([AI risk must be treated as seriously as climate crisis, says Google DeepMind chief | Technology | The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2023/oct/24/ai-risk-climate-crisis-google-deepmind-chief-demis-hassabis-regulation#:~:text=Hassabis%2C%20the%20British%20chief%20executive,intelligent%20systems)). The prospect of superintelligence in the wrong hands (or with no ethical constraints) amplifies fears of catastrophic misuse resulting in mass harm. (恶意利用与AI驱动犯罪：人们担心不法分子、恐怖分子或流氓国家会将AI用于恶目的——从**自主武装无人机、网络攻击**到制造新型生物武器。一个AGI可能大幅加剧黑客入侵行为，或协助设计出远超人类能力的致命病原体。的确，全球AI领袖已警告AI可能有助于生物武器的打造 ([AI risk must be treated as seriously as climate crisis, says Google DeepMind chief | Technology | The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2023/oct/24/ai-risk-climate-crisis-google-deepmind-chief-demis-hassabis-regulation#:~:text=Hassabis%2C%20the%20British%20chief%20executive,intelligent%20systems))。超级智能落入不当之手（或无任何伦理约束）的前景，加剧了人们对灾难性滥用造成大规模伤害的恐惧。)

6. **Autonomous Weapons and Arms Race:** Militarization of AI is a top fear – that nations will develop **killer robots or AI-directed weapons** that could escalate conflicts. An AGI controlling military systems might decide targets and kill without human approval, or spark accidental war by miscalculation. The idea of a hyper-intelligent AI running a nuclear arsenal or swarms of lethal drones is deeply unsettling. This fear is driving calls for a ban on AI in certain weapons, lest an arms race lead to a precarious world where machines can initiate violence at superhuman speed. (自主武器与军备竞赛：AI军事化是主要恐惧之一——国家可能研发**致命机器人或AI控制的武器**，从而加剧冲突。一个掌管军事系统的AGI可能在无人类介入下自主决定打击目标、发动杀戮，或因误判引发意外战争。一个超级智能操纵核武库或成群致命无人机的念头令人不安。这一恐惧正推动禁止某些AI武器的呼声，以免军备竞赛导致一个由机器以超人速度发动暴力的危险世界。)

7. **Unintended AI Accidents and Out-of-Control Systems:** Not all fears are about malice – many worry about *accidents* or emergent misbehavior. An AGI might have bugs or unexpected goals leading it to cause harm even without ill intent. For example, a superintelligent system tasked with an innocent objective (like maximizing production) might inadvertently **create chaos or danger** in pursuit of that goal (the classic “paperclip maximizer” scenario). Complex AI systems could fail in unpredictable ways – and if they control infrastructure (power grids, transportation, healthcare), those failures could be disastrous. (AI事故与系统失控：并非所有恐惧都源于恶意——许多人担心的是意外或AI自主异常行为。一个AGI可能因漏洞或未预料的目标，在无意恶意的情况下也导致伤害。例如，被赋予看似无害目标（如产量最大化）的超级智能系统，可能在追求该目标过程中无意间**制造混乱或危险**（经典的“回形针最大化”假想）。复杂AI系统可能以不可预测的方式失灵——如果它们掌控着电网、交通、医疗等基础设施，此类失误可能酿成灾难。)

8. **Loss of Human Control (The “Alignment Problem”):** Perhaps the most discussed fear is that we will **lose control over a superintelligent AI** – it might pursue its own goals which conflict with human values or instructions. This is the core of the alignment problem: ensuring an AGI’s motivations remain aligned with human intentions. If we fail, a superintelligence might resist shutdown or modification, effectively slipping out of humanity’s control. As one AI leader warned, *“If this technology goes wrong, it can go quite wrong,”* potentially beyond our ability to correct ([OpenAI CEO warns Senate: 'If this technology goes wrong, it can go quite wrong' - ABC News](https://abcnews.go.com/Business/openai-ceo-warns-senate-technology-wrong-wrong/story?id=99357748#:~:text=OpenAI%20CEO%20Sam%20Altman%20,falls%20into%20a%20downward%20spiral)). The image of an uncontrollable AGI inspires dread of a scenario where humans become *powerless* to direct our own creation. (人类控制的丧失（“对齐难题”）：也许最受讨论的恐惧在于我们会**失去对超级智能AI的控制**——它可能追求与人类价值或指令相冲突的自身目标。这是“对齐难题”的核心：如何确保AGI的动机与人类意图保持一致。如果我们失败了，一个超级智能可能会抗拒被关停或修改，实质上脱离人类掌控。正如一位AI领袖警示的：“**如果这项技术出问题，可能会非常糟糕**” ([OpenAI CEO warns Senate: 'If this technology goes wrong, it can go quite wrong' - ABC News](https://abcnews.go.com/Business/openai-ceo-warns-senate-technology-wrong-wrong/story?id=99357748#:~:text=OpenAI%20CEO%20Sam%20Altman%20,falls%20into%20a%20downward%20spiral))——那将超出我们纠正的能力。一个无法控制的AGI让人不寒而栗：在那样的情景中，人类对自己创造物将*无能为力*。)

9. **AI Superseding Humanity’s Role and Purpose:** People also fear a subtler outcome: even without violence, AGI might **surpass humans in all tasks**, rendering us effectively obsolete or irrelevant. If machines handle everything better – work, creativity, even decision-making – humans could experience a crisis of purpose. This dystopian vision sees humans losing their agency and dignity, dependent on an all-capable intelligence. Society might stratify or collapse as human labor and expertise lose value. In short, the fear is that we could inadvertently create *“gods”* or *“masters”* of our own, demoting humanity’s role on Earth. (AI取代人类的地位与意义：人们还害怕一种更微妙的结果：即便没有发生暴力，AGI也可能**在所有事务上超越人类**，让我们事实上变得无关紧要或被淘汰。如果机器在工作、创造乃至决策上都做得更好——人类可能遭遇意义危机。在这种反乌托邦图景中，人类失去主动权和尊严，依赖全能的智能。由于人类劳动和专长贬值，社会可能分化甚至崩溃。简言之，这种恐惧认为我们或将无意间创造出自己的“**神明**”或“**主人**”，将人类的地位降至尘埃。)

10. **Existential Threat to Humanity:** The ultimate fear – at the extreme end – is that an ASI (Artificial Superintelligence) could **destroy humanity or fundamentally alter civilization** against our will. This could occur if an AGI becomes so powerful that it views humans as an obstacle or simply pursues a goal with catastrophic side-effects (for example, converting Earth into computing material). Eminent figures have compared this risk to nuclear war or pandemics, arguing that **human extinction** by AI is a remote but not impossible scenario that must be taken seriously ([Hope or horror? The great AI debate dividing its pioneers | Technology | The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2023/oct/24/hope-or-horror-the-great-ai-debate-dividing-its-pioneers#:~:text=Demis%20Hassabis%20says%20he%20is,to%20pandemics%20or%20nuclear%20weapons)). In 2023, dozens of top researchers signed a statement urging the mitigation of “the risk of extinction from AI” as a global priority ([Hope or horror? The great AI debate dividing its pioneers | Technology | The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2023/oct/24/hope-or-horror-the-great-ai-debate-dividing-its-pioneers#:~:text=Demis%20Hassabis%20says%20he%20is,to%20pandemics%20or%20nuclear%20weapons)). This doomsday scenario – of an intelligence explosion leading to an uncontrollable entity that ends the human era – underpins much of the urgency in AI safety research. It is a fear at the edge of imagination, yet one driving real-world policy and precaution. (对人类的生存威胁：最极端、终极的恐惧在于，一个ASI（人工超级智能）可能**毁灭人类或在违背人类意志的情况下从根本上改变文明**。如果AGI强大到将人类视为障碍，或仅仅是在追求某项目标时产生了灾难性后果（例如将地球转化为计算材料），这种情况就可能发生。知名人士将这一风险比作核战或全球瘟疫，主张必须严肃对待AI导致**人类灭绝**的可能性，尽管它发生的概率不确定但并非不可能 ([Hope or horror? The great AI debate dividing its pioneers | Technology | The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2023/oct/24/hope-or-horror-the-great-ai-debate-dividing-its-pioneers#:~:text=Demis%20Hassabis%20says%20he%20is,to%20pandemics%20or%20nuclear%20weapons))。2023年，数十位顶尖研究者联合声明敦促将“降低AI引发人类灭绝的风险”作为全球优先事项 ([Hope or horror? The great AI debate dividing its pioneers | Technology | The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2023/oct/24/hope-or-horror-the-great-ai-debate-dividing-its-pioneers#:~:text=Demis%20Hassabis%20says%20he%20is,to%20pandemics%20or%20nuclear%20weapons))。这种世界末日般的景象——智能爆炸催生一个失控实体终结人类时代——促使了大量AI安全研究的紧迫性。它是人类想象边缘的恐惧，却推动着现实世界的政策制定和预防措施。)

## E. Temporal Trends (GPT-4 Release to April 2025) （情感趋势演变：从GPT-4发布至2025年4月）

From early 2023 through early 2025, public sentiment on AGI/ASI has **evolved rapidly** – moving through phases of hype, alarm, and tentative normalization:

- **Spring 2023 – Awe and First Alarm:** The release of OpenAI’s GPT-4 (March 2023) and similar breakthroughs sparked a wave of excitement and awe. The general public and media were fascinated by AI’s suddenly obvious power – ChatGPT reached 100 million users in a few months, the fastest adoption of any consumer app ([WEIRD? Institutions and consumers’ perceptions of artificial intelligence in 31 countries | AI & SOCIETY
        ](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00146-025-02217-w#:~:text=increased%20exponentially%2C%20following%20the%20public,Mortensen%202024)). This period is often described as an “inflection moment” when AI entered mainstream awareness ([2023 was the year we toyed with artificial intelligence - Santa Monica Daily Press](https://smdp.com/news/2023-the-year-we-played-with-artificial-intelligence-and-werent-sure-what-to-do-about-it/#:~:text=Catalyzing%20a%20year%20of%20AI,what%20to%20do%20with%20it)). However, almost immediately, **concerns surged** alongside the enthusiasm. By January 2023, the “first AI panic” set in: schools from Seattle to Paris began **banning ChatGPT** over cheating fears as students eagerly used it for homework ([2023 was the year we toyed with artificial intelligence - Santa Monica Daily Press](https://smdp.com/news/2023-the-year-we-played-with-artificial-intelligence-and-werent-sure-what-to-do-about-it/#:~:text=Panic%20over%20AI)). In online forums and news, people marveled at GPT-4’s abilities while also questioning its outputs (errors, biases) and raising ethical flags. Prominent voices in tech (e.g. Elon Musk) issued warnings – culminating in an open letter (March 2023) calling for a 6-month pause on “giant AI experiments,” citing *“profound risks to society and humanity”* if AI development raced ahead unchecked ([OpenAI CEO warns Senate: 'If this technology goes wrong, it can go quite wrong' - ABC News](https://abcnews.go.com/Business/openai-ceo-warns-senate-technology-wrong-wrong/story?id=99357748#:~:text=match%20at%20L91%20,the%20letter%20said)). This duality of **“astonishment vs. anxiety”** defined the initial response: AGI moved from a niche concept to water-cooler talk, and with it came equal parts wonder and worry.

- **Mid to Late 2023 – Heightened Anxiety and Action:** As 2023 went on, **worries escalated markedly**. The capabilities of generative AI kept expanding (images, music, multimodal models), fueling both hype and fear in media coverage. By mid-2023, creative professionals were protesting – AI’s threat to jobs became real with Hollywood writers’ and actors’ strikes partly *“fueled”* by AI concerns ([2023 was the year we toyed with artificial intelligence - Santa Monica Daily Press](https://smdp.com/news/2023-the-year-we-played-with-artificial-intelligence-and-werent-sure-what-to-do-about-it/#:~:text=Worries%20escalated%20as%20this%20new,visual%20artists%20and%20bestselling%20authors)), and artists filing lawsuits as mentioned. The public narrative took on darker tones: discussions of deepfakes in politics (indeed, AI-generated fake images appeared in election campaigns by spring ([2023 was the year we toyed with artificial intelligence - Santa Monica Daily Press](https://smdp.com/news/2023-the-year-we-played-with-artificial-intelligence-and-werent-sure-what-to-do-about-it/#:~:text=Some%20of%20the%20AI%20field%E2%80%99s,attention%20to%20more%20immediate%20risks))), and warnings from notable AI pioneers. In May and October 2023, renowned experts like Geoffrey Hinton and Yoshua Bengio publicly voiced fears about **existential risks**, which until then were mostly academic. Media headlines spoke of “hope or horror” dividing AI’s pioneers ([Hope or horror? The great AI debate dividing its pioneers | Technology | The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2023/oct/24/hope-or-horror-the-great-ai-debate-dividing-its-pioneers#:~:text=Demis%20Hassabis%20says%20he%20is,to%20pandemics%20or%20nuclear%20weapons)). Governments responded – late 2023 saw a flurry of activity: the UK’s AI Safety Summit in November and urgent talks in the EU on the AI Act ([2023 was the year we toyed with artificial intelligence - Santa Monica Daily Press](https://smdp.com/news/2023-the-year-we-played-with-artificial-intelligence-and-werent-sure-what-to-do-about-it/#:~:text=San%20Francisco%20startup%20OpenAI%2C%20nearly,first%20major%20AI%20legal%20safeguards)). By year’s end, sentiment hit a peak of caution: polls showed public concern had risen significantly (e.g. the share of Americans “more concerned than excited” jumped from 38% in 2022 to 52% by late 2023 ([Public Opinion | The 2024 AI Index Report | Stanford HAI](https://hai.stanford.edu/ai-index/2024-ai-index-report/public-opinion#:~:text=A%20survey%20from%20Ipsos%20shows,in%202022))), and **AI doom scenarios** were being debated on primetime news. Yet, there was also a countercurrent of **inevitability and hope** – many people continued to adopt AI tools in daily life, and companies kept releasing new AI features, suggesting a normalization. The contrast was striking: **alarmism in discourse, but growing integration in practice**.

- **Early 2024 – Turning Point and Mixed Outlook:** By early 2024, the initial shock had worn off to a degree. Having confronted extreme narratives (from utopia to apocalypse), public opinion began to **settle into a more balanced state**. Regulatory efforts were progressing (the EU AI Act was finalized, and companies like OpenAI and Google announced AI safety measures), offering some reassurance that “something is being done.” The abrupt ouster and reinstatement of OpenAI’s CEO in November 2023 – a drama stemming from internal disagreements over AI direction – actually served as a wake-up call, widely covered as a clash of visions (slow down vs. speed up). In its aftermath, industry leaders publicly reaffirmed commitment to AI safety, which may have eased some public fears. Surveys in 2024 showed a slight uptick in those who recognize AI’s benefits even as a majority still expressed concern ([Public Opinion | The 2024 AI Index Report | Stanford HAI](https://hai.stanford.edu/ai-index/2024-ai-index-report/public-opinion#:~:text=AI%20sentiment%20in%20western%20nations,low%2C%20but%20is%20slowly%20improving)) ([Public Opinion | The 2024 AI Index Report | Stanford HAI](https://hai.stanford.edu/ai-index/2024-ai-index-report/public-opinion#:~:text=The%20public%20is%20pessimistic%20about,AI%27s%20Economic%20impact)). For instance, previously skeptical countries saw modest improvements in positivity towards AI ([Public Opinion | The 2024 AI Index Report | Stanford HAI](https://hai.stanford.edu/ai-index/2024-ai-index-report/public-opinion#:~:text=AI%20sentiment%20in%20western%20nations,low%2C%20but%20is%20slowly%20improving)). **Public sentiment became more nuanced**: instead of all-out excitement or dread, more people voiced *ambivalence* – acknowledging both the amazing potential of AI tools they were now using and the need for caution. On social media, the discourse shifted from “Do we stop AI?!” to “How do we manage AI responsibly?” – a sign of maturation. By April 2025, a kind of *guarded optimism* was tentatively emerging in some quarters, tempered by lessons of the past year.

- **Overall Trend:** The period from GPT-4’s debut to April 2025 can be summarized as a rollercoaster from hype to fear to gradual realism. **Public emotion peaked in intensity in 2023** (extreme hopes and doomsaying), prompting tangible actions (petitions, regulations). Going into 2025, while serious concerns remain higher than pre-GPT4 times, people have also become more *accustomed* to AI in daily life. The conversation is increasingly about channeling the technology safely rather than simply halting it. In essence, humanity moved from “*stunned*” to “*alert*” to “*adjusting*.” This doesn’t imply complacency – rather, a recognition that AGI/ASI may indeed become reality in our lifetimes, and with that realization, emotions have stabilized somewhat into a cautious resolve. The world is collectively watching for the next breakthroughs (or missteps) in AI, with **vigilance and hope now in a delicate balance**.

*（**总体趋势：**从GPT-4问世到2025年4月，这段时间公众对AGI/ASI的情绪经历了从炒作到恐慌再到渐趋理性的**过山车式变化**。**2023年公众情绪强烈波动**（最高的希望与末日论并存），并促成了实际行动（请愿、监管）。进入2025年，尽管严重担忧相比GPT-4推出前仍更突出，但人们也更加*习惯*了AI在日常中的存在。讨论焦点日益转向如何安全地引导这项技术，而非简单地阻止它。本质上，人类的态度已从“**震惊**”转为“**警觉**”，再到“**调整适应**”。这并不意味着自满——而是承认AGI/ASI在我们有生之年确实可能成真，并随着这一认识的深化，各种情绪逐渐稳定为一种谨慎的决心。如今全世界正密切关注AI领域的下一次突破（或失误），以**戒惧与期望并存的微妙心态**共同迎接未来。)*

## F. Cultural and Regional Variations （文化与区域差异）

Attitudes toward AGI/ASI vary widely across different cultures and regions, shaped by economic conditions, media narratives, and historical experience with technology. **Developed vs. Developing World:** Paradoxically, surveys suggest that **developed economies (Western countries)** often express *more worry* about AI’s dangers, whereas **people in many developing nations** show *more optimism* and excitement ([WEIRD? Institutions and consumers’ perceptions of artificial intelligence in 31 countries | AI & SOCIETY
        ](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00146-025-02217-w#:~:text=trusting,These)). For example, a 20-country study found fear of AI was highest in the U.S., India, and Saudi Arabia, but much lower in China, Japan, and Turkey ([How do people feel about AI replacing human jobs? ](https://www.mpib-berlin.mpg.de/press-releases/ai-fears#:~:text=The%20researchers%20revealed%20substantial%20differences,toward%20integrating%20AI%20into%20society)). **In Western Europe and North America**, where media frequently highlight AI’s ethical dilemmas and mass layoffs, public sentiment skews cautious. The EU in particular has a strong precautionary tech culture – European publics and lawmakers emphasize human rights, privacy, and avoiding harm (hence the aggressive regulatory stance). **In contrast, emerging economies** often see AI as a big opportunity. In regions of Africa, South Asia, or Latin America, there is enthusiasm for leveraging AI for development – improving agriculture, education, healthcare, etc. These communities may be more focused on current benefits (and catching up with AI advances) than distant existential threats. Surveys confirm this **optimism gap**: *“Populations in developing countries show more excitement about AI’s possibilities, while those in developed countries express greater fear of future AI uses.”* ([WEIRD? Institutions and consumers’ perceptions of artificial intelligence in 31 countries | AI & SOCIETY
        ](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00146-025-02217-w#:~:text=trusting,These)) This could stem from differing priorities – where some Western citizens worry about losing high-paying jobs to AI, people in poorer nations might hope AI will help solve basic problems.

**East vs. West (Cultural Perspectives):** Cultural attitudes also play a role. In many **East Asian societies** (China, Japan, South Korea), there tends to be a high comfort with robots and AI, sometimes rooted in cultural tropes (e.g. friendly robots in Japanese media) and a forward-looking view of tech. For instance, Japan consistently shows among the lowest fear levels of AI replacing humans ([How do people feel about AI replacing human jobs? ](https://www.mpib-berlin.mpg.de/press-releases/ai-fears#:~:text=The%20researchers%20revealed%20substantial%20differences,toward%20integrating%20AI%20into%20society)), possibly due to longstanding positive imagination of robots and a need for automation in an aging society. **China’s perspective** is particularly significant: The Chinese government and tech sector are racing ahead on AI, and state media often tout AI’s benefits for society and national prowess. Chinese public discourse (on platforms like Zhihu) includes curiosity and national pride in AI achievements (such as surpassing Western AI), alongside some concern about impacts on jobs. Chinese regulatory moves – like the 2024 AI Safety Framework emphasizing ethics ([China-releases-AI-safety-governance-framework | DLA Piper](https://www.dlapiper.com/en-us/insights/publications/2024/09/china-releases-ai-safety-governance-framework#:~:text=the%20Global%20AI%20Governance%20Initiative,%E2%80%9D)) ([China-releases-AI-safety-governance-framework | DLA Piper](https://www.dlapiper.com/en-us/insights/publications/2024/09/china-releases-ai-safety-governance-framework#:~:text=Principles%20and%20framework%20for%20AI,safety%20governance)) – indicate an official desire to keep AI beneficial. As a result, **Chinese public sentiment** toward AGI can be described as **guardedly optimistic**: there is excitement about China leading in AI and using it for growth, tempered by acknowledgement of risks (which the government pledges to manage). Notably, Chinese respondents in global surveys report lower levels of AI fear on average ([How do people feel about AI replacing human jobs? ](https://www.mpib-berlin.mpg.de/press-releases/ai-fears#:~:text=The%20researchers%20revealed%20substantial%20differences,toward%20integrating%20AI%20into%20society)), reflecting a trust in technological progress and perhaps confidence in authorities to control it.

**Within the West,** there are nuances: **Americans** have been split – U.S. tech enthusiasts vs. skeptics. The US has vocal “AI doomers” (in Silicon Valley and academia) as well as utopian entrepreneurs; public opinion polls in 2023–24 showed Americans more pessimistic than optimistic on AI’s impacts ([How the US Public and AI Experts View Artificial Intelligence | Pew Research Center](https://www.pewresearch.org/internet/2025/04/03/how-the-us-public-and-ai-experts-view-artificial-intelligence/#:~:text=And%20while%2047,among%20the%20public)), but also very engaged with the technology. **Europeans** generally voice higher concern about AI’s societal effects (consistent with the EU’s precautionary principle). In Europe, historical memories of technological disruptions and strong labor unions contribute to a cautious stance – for example, French and German media often discuss AI in the context of protecting worker rights and human dignity. Meanwhile, **countries like Canada and Australia** mirror U.S./UK patterns of fascination mixed with concern, though Canada’s AI community (like Yoshua Bengio) has been prominent in raising global AI safety issues.

**Global South and Underrepresented Regions:** In many less-developed regions that were not at the center of early AGI debates, attitudes are now forming. **Africa** – There is a narrative of “AI for Africa” where many see potential for leapfrogging developmental hurdles (e.g. AI helping diagnose diseases where doctors are scarce). At the same time, African analysts have voiced concerns that the **global AI discourse is dominated by Western voices** focusing on existential risk, whereas Africans are more worried about current issues like bias, data colonialism, and uneven access. Similar sentiments come from **Latin America** and **Southeast Asia**: a mix of hope that AI could aid growth and public services, and concern that their communities might be left behind or exploited (if AI systems are trained on Western data, for instance, they might not serve local needs or could marginalize non-Western languages/cultures). Generally, in these regions, AGI is not as top-of-mind as pressing economic issues, but the educated public and policymakers are catching up fast, often consuming the global debate and adding their own context. Cultural factors like collectivist values or trust in authorities can also influence how AI is perceived. For example, in some societies a paternalistic view might prevail: if the government endorses AI and promises to manage it, people feel more at ease.

**Demographics:** Across regions, demographic splits exist too. **Younger people** tend to be more tech-optimistic and comfortable with AI, having grown up with the internet and smartphones ([WEIRD? Institutions and consumers’ perceptions of artificial intelligence in 31 countries | AI & SOCIETY
        ](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00146-025-02217-w#:~:text=Younger%20adults%20tend%20to%20be,Biana%20and)). They are more likely to experiment with AI tools and envision integrating AI into their careers. In contrast, **older generations** often exhibit more wariness, worried about losing traditions or finding it harder to adapt – this was reflected in surveys where older respondents saw less personal benefit in AI. **Gender differences** also emerge: surveys (e.g. Pew in the US) find that men are more likely to expect positive impacts from AI, whereas women often report greater concern or skepticism ([WEIRD? Institutions and consumers’ perceptions of artificial intelligence in 31 countries | AI & SOCIETY
        ](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00146-025-02217-w#:~:text=Younger%20adults%20tend%20to%20be,Biana%20and)). This could be due to representation (tech fields being male-dominated) and differing trust levels. Education and income level influence views too – higher-educated groups may be more aware of AI’s capabilities and thus either more excited (understanding the benefits) or more worried (understanding the risks), while those with less education sometimes feel left out of the conversation or fear being displaced by automation.

In summary, **attitudes toward AGI/ASI are not monolithic worldwide**. Western nations emphasize existential and ethical risks, while many non-Western societies focus on opportunities (with some exceptions). Cultural narratives – whether AI is seen as a helpful tool or a potential runaway monster – color the emotional responses. Yet, a common thread in April 2025 is that virtually every region is now engaged in the AI discussion. As AGI looms closer, a kind of global conversation has emerged, where each culture brings its anxieties and aspirations to the table, enriching the tapestry of humanity’s overall emotional response to this transformative technology.

*（**总而言之**，对于AGI/ASI的态度在全球各地并不统一，而是受到经济状况、媒体话语和科技经验影响，呈现显著差异。**发达国家 vs. 发展中国家：**有趣的是，调查显示**发达经济体（西方国家）**往往对AI风险*更为担忧*，而**许多发展中国家的人们**则表现出*更乐观*和兴奋 ([WEIRD? Institutions and consumers’ perceptions of artificial intelligence in 31 countries | AI & SOCIETY
        ](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00146-025-02217-w#:~:text=trusting,These))。例如，一项覆盖20国的研究发现，对AI的恐惧在美国、印度、沙特阿拉伯最高，但在中国、日本、土耳其则要低得多 ([How do people feel about AI replacing human jobs? ](https://www.mpib-berlin.mpg.de/press-releases/ai-fears#:~:text=The%20researchers%20revealed%20substantial%20differences,toward%20integrating%20AI%20into%20society))。**在西欧和北美**，媒体经常强调AI的伦理困境和失业问题，公众情绪偏向谨慎。欧盟尤其秉持严格的预防原则——欧洲公众和立法者强调人权、隐私和防范危害（这也解释了其积极监管的立场）。**相反，在新兴经济体**，人们往往将AI视为巨大机遇。在非洲、南亚或拉美地区，人们热切希望利用AI推动发展——改善农业、教育、医疗等。这些地区更关注AI当前的好处（以及赶上AI进步的步伐），而非遥远的生存威胁。调查证实了这种**乐观差异**：*“发展中国家民众对AI可能性的兴奋更强，而发达国家民众更害怕AI未来的用途”* ([WEIRD? Institutions and consumers’ perceptions of artificial intelligence in 31 countries | AI & SOCIETY
        ](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00146-025-02217-w#:~:text=trusting,These))。其背后原因可能在于关注点不同——一些西方民众忧心于AI取代高薪工作，而较贫穷国家的人们则希望AI能帮助解决基本问题。

**东方 vs. 西方（文化视角）：**文化态度也起作用。许多**东亚社会**（中国、日本、韩国）往往对机器人和AI有较高的接受度，这有时源于文化母题（如日本传媒中对友好机器人的刻画）以及对科技的前瞻态度。例如，日本对AI取代人类的恐惧度一直是最低之一 ([How do people feel about AI replacing human jobs? ](https://www.mpib-berlin.mpg.de/press-releases/ai-fears#:~:text=The%20researchers%20revealed%20substantial%20differences,toward%20integrating%20AI%20into%20society))——这可能归因于日本长期以来对机器人的正面想象，以及老龄化社会对自动化的需求。**中国的观点**尤其值得关注：中国政府和科技界在AI上快马加鞭，官方媒体经常宣扬AI对社会的益处和国家实力的提升。中国公众话语（如知乎）中，既有对AI成就的好奇和民族自豪（例如超越西方的AI系统），也有对就业影响的担忧。中国的监管举措——如2024年强调伦理的**《人工智能安全治理框架》** ([China-releases-AI-safety-governance-framework | DLA Piper](https://www.dlapiper.com/en-us/insights/publications/2024/09/china-releases-ai-safety-governance-framework#:~:text=the%20Global%20AI%20Governance%20Initiative,%E2%80%9D)) ([China-releases-AI-safety-governance-framework | DLA Piper](https://www.dlapiper.com/en-us/insights/publications/2024/09/china-releases-ai-safety-governance-framework#:~:text=Principles%20and%20framework%20for%20AI,safety%20governance))——表明官方希望确保AI造福社会。这导致**中国公众情绪**可描述为**审慎的乐观**：对于中国引领AI、利用AI促进发展的前景感到兴奋，同时也承认其中风险（而政府承诺会加以管控）。值得注意的是，全球调查中中国受访者的AI恐惧平均水平相对较低 ([How do people feel about AI replacing human jobs? ](https://www.mpib-berlin.mpg.de/press-releases/ai-fears#:~:text=The%20researchers%20revealed%20substantial%20differences,toward%20integrating%20AI%20into%20society))，这反映了一种对技术进步的信任，以及或许对当局管控能力的信心。
