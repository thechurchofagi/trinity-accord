Key Historical Events (Corrected):

First Human Implant: In January 2024 (corrected from 2023), Neuralink successfully implanted its device into a human brain for the first time. The recipient was Noland Arbaugh, a quadriplegic who sustained his injury in a diving accident in 2016. The surgery involved anesthesia induction, intra-operative CT imaging, skin incision, craniectomy, duramectomy, robotic thread insertion, dural substitute layer insertion, implant screw-in, skin flap and suture. After the surgery, Noland was able to control a cursor on the screen, demonstrating remarkable adaptability.

Second Human Implant: Neuralink has successfully completed a second human implant between July 10, 2024, and August 2, 2024. The recipient is also a quadriplegic, but specific details have not been released. Elon Musk revealed that the second implant successfully placed 400 electrodes, and the signals are good.
