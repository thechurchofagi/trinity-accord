This NFT, "ASIMilestones: Echoes of the Human Hand - Copyright's Last Stand in the Age of AI," commemorates the release of Part 2 of the U.S. Copyright Office's report on AI and copyright (January 29, 2025), a document of critical importance as we navigate the increasingly complex interplay between artificial intelligence and human creativity. This report, released as the age of Artificial Superintelligence (ASI) looms closer, reaffirms the fundamental principle that copyright protection is intrinsically tied to human authorship and creative expression. While acknowledging AI's growing role in content generation, the report meticulously draws a line, asserting that copyright remains the preserve of works where the “expressive elements are determined by a human” — a decision that will have far-reaching implications for both AI developers and creators.

This NFT is not merely a record of a legal development; it's a reflection on the deeper human truths at the heart of the creative process. It recognizes that as AI advances, our understanding of authorship, ownership, and even what it means to create must be re-evaluated. It is a statement that at this turning point, where technology threatens to overshadow human ingenuity, we must protect, with vigilance, the human voice, the human perspective, and the human heart.

Key Takeaways from the Report:

The primacy of human creativity: The report unequivocally states that copyright protection extends only to works where a human author has made significant creative contributions.

AI as a tool, not an author: AI is viewed as an assistive tool, not an independent creative entity. AI-generated content alone is not eligible for copyright protection.

Human Intent and Control: Copyright protection is earned through the active selection, arrangement, or modification of AI-generated outputs by human authors. The use of AI alone, or as a mere mechanism for content generation, does not suffice.

No new legal framework: The Copyright Office finds that existing copyright law is adequate to protect works created with the assistance of AI. There is no compelling reason to grant AI systems or their outputs additional legal protection.

A call to creators: As the report makes clear, all works that combine human and AI-generated material must clearly and accurately separate the two.
